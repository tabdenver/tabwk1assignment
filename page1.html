    Describing the purpose of IT in modern business


    Information Technology is a vital part of a modern business’ structure, whether that is; data collection of employees, payroll, content data of business practice. This can be achieved on-site or being geographically mobile and communication to other businesses and across different communications platforms like software and devices.

    This inception only enhances business practice as it’s not dependent on how small or large scale the company is. As a multi-national may need information from a small business and the best way to collate, send, receive information will vary through IT solutions such as; laptops, computers, information stored in servers, access to smart phones with communication devices connected to email applications by using different software packages ranging from specially designed tools to ones used by public and in general use for best business practices.

    The security aspect of sensitive data or the choice to make information made available to the public allows flexibility when it’s accessed by the internet.  Research and data can be stored, regularly being accessed and updated and backed-up to servers and exist within ‘virtual online clouds’ that allow businesses to keep their ideas and strategies away more securely than paper footprints which are more easily damaged, lost or miss-placed.   

    The wide range of what consists within IT ranges across different platforms such as mobile banking apps and websites which allow users to access their information at any time from anywhere with internet access, securely by using passwords allowing them to make financial transactions.
